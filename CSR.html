Credit Score Reports

Understanding the credit concept for any borrower and lender is an important consideration for the lending process. This topic is not only important for people seeking a physician loan, but for anyone looking for financing. The three major repositories are TransUnion, Experian, and Equifax. These firms send information from your creditors in a format that shows the mane of the creditor, the origination of the dept, the high balance amount, the current balance amount, and the payment history. This data merged into a single report so that duplicate accounts are omitted, and lenders see how much debt a potential borrower has, and how has that borrower paid those charges back over time. This payment history has a significant impact on one credit score. Each of the credit agencies provide a credit risk score that lenders evaluate in the underwriting process. Typically, credit scores over 720 are deemed acceptable for lending. That, however, is not the only factor. Some firms require a minimum active trade line history of 2 years and require at least 4 open active accounts. This history may provide lenders with a better understanding of a potential borrowers’ likelihood of timely payments. Some physician lenders will consider loans with credit scores under 720, however, you might expect to be offered a higher interest rate due to the additional default risk. People can contact the credit agencies to order their credit report on their own.
